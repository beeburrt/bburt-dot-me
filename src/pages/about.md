## My Story

It's hard to spin drug and alcohol addiction into something positive. But that's what I'm attempting here.

I wasn't always a dirtbag. I grew up in a good family, went to church, did good in school. For some reason, I've always been drawn toward the wrong kind of people. The wrong kind of places and things.

I was into skateboarding and punk rock shows (not that those are wrong, lol). I spent my time hanging out with friends and generally up to no good. Along the way I fell into drinking and doing drugs.

I call it self-medicating for my depression and anxiety.

Over the years I tried pretty much everything. In my thirties I started using heroin. Soon I started selling it to support my habit.

Opiate addiction is horrible. I wouldn't wish it on my enemies (I don't have any enemies, lol). Ask anyone who has lived through it and I guarantee they know multiple people who have died from an overdose.

I've made a lot of mistakes in my life. But my biggest by far was not calling the authorities when I found my friend's body. I was stupid, strung-out and scared. I just left. I'm sorry.

I paid for it when they sentenced me to 60 months for Controlled Substance Homicide.

You would think that that would do the trick. That I would stay on the right track after that. And I had intended on staying clean from then on, but I guess I had more figuring it out to do.

I went back to prison in 2020 for a meth induced, psychotic episode that ended with me thinking the second-coming of Jesus Christ was in full effect and so I think I thought that nothing I did mattered anymore. I ended up ramming a sheriff's car with my boss's truck.

I'm still paying for that one.

But while I was locked up this last time, I had the idea that I wanted to try learning programming again. I had started learning Python a long time ago, in an intro to Computer Science class that I never finished.

But so when I got out, I found a Dell laptop on Craigslist for $200. It's actually a good computer for that price. Intel i5 with 8G RAM and a 256G SSD.

Anyways, I got this computer and now I spend as much time as I can trying to learn as much as I can.
